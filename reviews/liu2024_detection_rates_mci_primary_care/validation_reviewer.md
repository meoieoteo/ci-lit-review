# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Validation of detection-rate framework and reporting quality.

## Bottom Line

Useful claims-scale evidence but not a validated clinical prediction or phenotyping algorithm.

## Validation Design Summary

HRS-derived expected-rate model applied to Medicare clinician/practice panels.

## Development and Validation Separation

HRS model derivation and 2016 validation are separated as described.

## Internal Validation

Prediction accuracy measures are reported for HRS-derived model.

## Temporal Validation

No temporal external validation.

## External Validation

No chart/cognitive-test validation in Medicare.

## Transportability

Transportable to U.S. Medicare primary care; local Epic implementation requires patient attribution.

## Calibration

Expected rates are model-based; main clinical calibration is not applicable.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

Clinician/practice rates are classified relative to expected using confidence intervals.

## Clinical Utility

The paper argues for improved primary-care detection but does not test an intervention.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Specialty-level differences reported; more subgroup fairness detail would be useful.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

Expected-rate uncertainty is discussed; code-label validation remains a gap.

## Reporting Completeness

Good main reporting.

## Reproducibility

Reproducible with Medicare/HRS access and supplement.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Strong for diagnosis-opportunity design, especially PCP contact/follow-up.

## Strengths

Large clinician/practice sample and explicit observed-to-expected inference.

## Concerns

MCI code definition may be incomplete and unvalidated.

## Missing Information

Detailed supplement and local validation.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Directly supports Section 5 gap on primary-care detection opportunity.

## Recommended Follow-up

Use with Tzeng to define PCP/AWV/specialty opportunity variables.
