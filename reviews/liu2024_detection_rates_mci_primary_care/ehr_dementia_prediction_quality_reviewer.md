# Specialist Review: EHR Dementia Prediction Quality

## Specialist Scope

Assessment of claims-derived MCI detection quality.

## Bottom Line

label_or_validation_limited and methodologically informative. Not a prediction model, but central for weak coded labels.

## Prediction Task Clarity

No prediction task.

## Index Date, Observation Window, and Prediction Horizon

2017-2019 detection window is clear.

## Data Source and Cohort Definition

100% Medicare claims/encounters, attributed primary care panels.

## Outcome and Reference Standard

Claims-coded MCI versus HRS-derived expected rates.

## Comparator or Control Group

Expected rates at clinician/practice panel level.

## EHR Text and Structured Data Use

Structured claims only.

## Missing Data and Feature Availability

Enrollment and attribution requirements may select patients with care contact.

## Validation

Expected model validation reported; no direct MCI code validation.

## Calibration, Thresholds, and Clinical Utility

No risk-score calibration; detection-rate threshold of 1 is the conceptual comparator.

## Reporting and Reproducibility

Good; supplement needed for full details.

## Care-Pathway and Downstream-Action Bias

Central and direct.

## Implementation Readiness

Useful for study design, not a clinical model.

## Strengths

Clinician/practice lens and national Medicare scope.

## Concerns

MCI documentation is the label and the failure mode.

## Missing Information

Validation of MCI claims code definition.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Shows future MCI diagnosis can reflect provider detection practices.
