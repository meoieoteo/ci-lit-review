# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Assessment of expected-rate model validation, subgroup robustness, and claims-label limitations.

## Bottom Line

Useful validation of an expected-vs-observed framework, but not a clinical prediction model or gold-standard dementia validation.

## Validation Design Summary

Expected-rate model developed with HRS 2000-2014 and assessed using 2016 HRS participants.

## Development and Validation Separation

Calibration/development and 2016 assessment are separated.

## Internal Validation

HRS model performance is reported; MCI AUROC is modest.

## Temporal Validation

Limited to later HRS wave assessment; not prospective clinical validation.

## External Validation

No external clinical validation of claims labels.

## Transportability

High for U.S. Medicare claims; limited for non-Medicare and local Epic populations.

## Calibration

Predicted and observed HRS rates are compared; formal calibration detail is limited in main text.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

Not a clinical thresholding study.

## Clinical Utility

Supports need for improved detection; no intervention utility analysis.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Strong subgroup reporting by race/ethnicity, dual eligibility, age, sex, and coverage type.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

Uses modified and original CCW dementia algorithms; supplemental details should be checked.

## Reporting Completeness

Good main reporting; supplement needed for exact code lists.

## Reproducibility

Reproducible with Medicare/HRS access and supplement details.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Strong warning about outcome ascertainment and PPV/NPV interpretation.

## Strengths

Very large population, national scope, expected-vs-observed framework.

## Concerns

Model-derived expected rates and claims-label misclassification.

## Missing Information

Detailed calibration and supplementary code definitions.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use for label validity and diagnosis-opportunity sections.

## Recommended Follow-up

Source-check supplement before exact quantitative manuscript claims.
