# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

ICU mortality and prolonged length-of-stay labels.

## Bottom Line

Labels are clear for ICU benchmarking but irrelevant to dementia target construction.

## Label Summary

Binary mortality and prolonged length of stay.

## Target Construct

`other`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

MIMIC-III outcomes.

## Index Date and Label Timing

ICU admission with first 24 hours as predictor window.

## Positive Case Definition

Death or prolonged stay according to paper definitions.

## Negative or Control Definition

Survival or non-prolonged stay.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Not central to dementia review.

## Label Validity Evidence

Mortality is objective; PLOS is operational.

## Misclassification Risks

PLOS can reflect discharge practice and nonclinical constraints.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

PLOS is strongly affected by care processes.

## Leakage Risks

First-24-hour notes should precede outcomes, but early outcomes require timing checks.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Low for labels.

## Strengths

Explicit observation window.

## Concerns

Benchmark labels do not map to cognitive care.

## Missing Information

Exact PLOS threshold and exclusions should be checked before citing.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use for method evaluation, not target design.

## Recommended Follow-up

No dementia-label follow-up.
