# Consolidated Review: ruan2025_icu_multimodal_notes

## Consolidation Status

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Ruan 2025 is a preprint methods analogy for uncertainty-aware multimodal EHR-note fusion, not dementia evidence.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

Use for method context around multimodal fusion and reliability metrics such as Brier score and NLL.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`longitudinal_ehr_modeling`; `calibration_or_thresholding`; `background_only`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

Do not cite for dementia prediction, cognitive note concepts, cardiology workflows, or validated clinical utility.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

Indirect and lower publication weight because it is an arXiv preprint.

## Evidence Strength

`useful_analogy`; `lower_publication_weight`; `background_context`

## Key Contributions

Belief-function/evidential multimodal fusion, structured and text encoders, first-24-hour ICU window, and reliability metrics.

## Key Limitations

Preprint status, ICU target mismatch, no external validation, no dementia label.

## Agreements Across Reviewers

All reviewers agree it is method background only.

## Tensions or Disagreements Across Reviewers

None.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

Mortality/PLOS labels do not inform dementia labels; PLOS is care-process sensitive.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

Internal benchmark with multiple seeds is useful, but external transport is absent.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

ICU treatment and discharge practices shape outcomes.

## Implications for Study Design

Consider reporting reliability/calibration metrics, not just AUROC/AUPRC, for multimodal models.

## Claims to Carry Forward

Uncertainty-aware multimodal fusion can be evaluated with discrimination and reliability metrics.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

Preprint ICU benchmark results should not be generalized to dementia.

## Open Questions

Would evidential fusion improve calibrated dementia risk estimates compared with simpler fusion?

## Recommended Next Action

Use only for methods background and metric selection.
