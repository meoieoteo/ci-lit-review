# Consolidated Review: bynum2024_regional_variation_diagnostic_intensity_dementia

## Consolidation Status

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Bynum et al. provides direct peer-reviewed evidence that ADRD diagnosis is partly a diagnosis-opportunity process, varying substantially by geography and subgroup after adjustment for measured population risk.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

This should be a central Section 5 citation. It gives the review a concrete term and operational example: ADRD-specific diagnostic intensity.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`downstream_action_bias`; `label_construction`; `problem_framing`; `implementation_or_transportability`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

EHR text signal, cardiology workflow, causal proof of why regions differ, or validated true dementia incidence.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

Peer-reviewed journal article. Strong direct relevance for diagnosis-opportunity bias; moderate evidence for exact mechanisms because the study is observational and claims-based.

## Evidence Strength

`strong_direct_evidence`; `methodological_caution`

## Key Contributions

- Defines and estimates ADRD-specific diagnosis intensity.
- Shows large regional variation in new ADRD diagnosis.
- Shows greater variation for younger-old, Black, and Hispanic beneficiaries.

## Key Limitations

Claims outcome, fee-for-service-only cohort, residual confounding, and no direct adjudication of true dementia status.

## Agreements Across Reviewers

All reviews agree that the paper is highly relevant to Section 5 and not relevant to NLP concept extraction.

## Tensions or Disagreements Across Reviewers

No major tension. The main caution is not to call low diagnostic intensity proven underdiagnosis without a gold standard.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

New claims-coded ADRD diagnosis is an observed label, not the same as disease onset. A one-year lookback helps define new diagnosis but does not solve disease-state uncertainty.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

The concept transports better than the exact HRR measure. A local project needs local diagnosis-opportunity variables.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

This paper directly supports treating future coded dementia as affected by access, practice norms, and diagnostic effort.

## Implications for Study Design

If future coded dementia is used as an outcome, model or stratify follow-up and diagnosis opportunity: PCP contact, specialist contact, cognitive testing, geography/access, death, and censoring.

## Claims to Carry Forward

- Diagnosis opportunity varies geographically.
- Diagnostic intensity differs most in subgroups already vulnerable to delayed diagnosis.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

- Regional differences are not automatically underdiagnosis or overdiagnosis.
- The study does not determine the causal mechanism of variation.

## Open Questions

Which local variables best approximate diagnosis intensity in our EHR data?

Promote to Section 5 supporting evidence in the next state update.
