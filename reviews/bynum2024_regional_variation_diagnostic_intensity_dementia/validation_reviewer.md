# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Validation and transportability of an observational diagnostic-intensity study.

## Bottom Line

Strong for describing regional variation in claims diagnosis intensity; limited for causal mechanism or true disease validation.

## Validation Design Summary

No prediction model validation. The study uses model adjustment, regional comparisons, and individual probability models.

## Development and Validation Separation

Not applicable.

## Internal Validation

Not applicable as prediction validation. Robustness is mainly through adjustment and subgroup analyses.

## Temporal Validation

No temporal validation beyond the 2018 lookback and 2019 new-diagnosis window.

## External Validation

No external validation dataset.

## Transportability

Transportable conceptually to Medicare claims and regional health-services analyses. Direct transport to local Epic encounter-level work requires local diagnosis-opportunity variables.

## Calibration

Not applicable as risk-score calibration.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

Not applicable.

## Clinical Utility

The paper suggests areas with low diagnostic intensity could be targeted for surveillance, but it does not test interventions or outcomes.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Strong. Analyses stratify by age and race/ethnicity and identify larger variation among younger-old, Black, and Hispanic beneficiaries.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

The study adjusts progressively for demographic, education, health risk, and general diagnosis intensity. Residual confounding remains possible.

## Reporting Completeness

Good for cohort, time windows, regional unit, and methods. Exact code details require supplement/source checks.

## Reproducibility

Likely reproducible with Medicare claims and regional datasets, subject to access and supplement details.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Supports validation planning by warning that future coded diagnosis may vary by region and subgroup.

## Strengths

- Large national sample.
- Clear time window.
- Direct diagnosis-intensity construct.

## Concerns

- Fee-for-service only.
- Observational residual confounding.
- No direct disease-state validation.

## Missing Information

Exact code lists and supplement content were not locally reviewed.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use for diagnosis-opportunity and fairness/transportability concerns in outcome ascertainment.

## Recommended Follow-up

Before manuscript use, source-check supplemental code lists and cited claims-algorithm validation.
