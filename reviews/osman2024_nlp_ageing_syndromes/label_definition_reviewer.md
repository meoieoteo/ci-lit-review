# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope
Assessment of label and reference-standard lessons from the systematic review of NLP for ageing syndromes.

## Bottom Line
`usable_with_caveats`. The review supports feasibility of NLP phenotyping but shows that target and reference-standard quality vary across primary studies.

## Label Summary
Included labels cover falls, dementia, delirium, incontinence, sarcopenia, frailty, and related ageing syndromes.

## Target Construct
- `current_undocumented_cognitive_impairment` for some dementia studies by analogy.
- `frailty_or_functional_status`
- other ageing syndromes.

## Label Source and Operational Definition
Common gold standards include manual text review by clinicians or trained annotators, with some studies using administrative codes or validated administrative algorithms.

## Index Date and Label Timing
Not standardized. The review focuses on identification of existing syndromes and excludes future-onset prediction.

## Positive Case Definition
Varies by included study.

## Negative or Control Definition
Varies by included study and requires primary-study extraction.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases
Not consistently extractable from the review summary.

## Label Validity Evidence
Manual review is common, but external validation is rare and QUADAS-2 concerns remain in many studies.

## Misclassification Risks
Broad syndrome definitions, variable note types, and inconsistent reference standards can create false positives and false negatives.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias
The review does not deeply address diagnosis opportunity. For dementia, this remains a major unresolved issue.

## Leakage Risks
Less central because the review excludes future prediction; individual studies still need timing checks.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring
Useful for current syndrome identification and NLP feasibility, not future risk or cardiology-specific labels.

## Strengths
- Systematic scope.
- Includes dementia and delirium.
- Highlights reference standards and diagnostic accuracy.

## Concerns
- Broad syndrome scope.
- Dementia-specific label details need extraction from primary studies.
- Future prediction excluded.

## Missing Information
Primary-study details for dementia note types, controls, reference standards, and external validation.

## Implications for This Literature Review
Use as background for feasibility and quality gaps in ageing-syndrome NLP. Do not use as a direct label recipe.

## Recommended Follow-up
Identify dementia-specific primary studies from this review for intake.
