# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope
Assessment of validation and reporting lessons from the systematic review of ageing-syndrome NLP.

## Bottom Line
`underreported_validation`. The review shows NLP feasibility but also substantial validation limitations.

## Validation Design Summary
Twenty-two studies were included. Reported sensitivity, specificity, PPV, and F1 varied widely. Only four studies had external validation.

## Development and Validation Separation
Variable across included studies.

## Internal Validation
Common but heterogeneous.

## Temporal Validation
Not a central finding.

## External Validation
Weak. Only four included studies externally validated their NLP algorithms.

## Transportability
Limited. Note types, care settings, syndromes, and documentation cultures vary across included studies.

## Calibration
Not central to diagnostic NLP extraction.

## Thresholds and Operating Points
Diagnostic accuracy metrics are summarized, but action-linked thresholds are not the focus.

## Clinical Utility
Not established for cardiology workflow or future cognitive-risk scoring.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation
Not a central extracted finding.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses
Heterogeneity prevented meta-analysis; synthesis was narrative.

## Reporting Completeness
Mixed. The review followed PRISMA and used QUADAS-2, but included studies often lacked consistent reporting.

## Reproducibility
Limited by heterogeneity and lack of standard NLP taxonomy.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring
Useful as validation caution for note-derived geriatric syndrome phenotyping.

## Strengths
- Systematic review methods.
- Quality assessment.
- Explicit external-validation finding.

## Concerns
- Few external validations.
- Broad target set.
- No future-prediction focus.

## Missing Information
Detailed dementia-specific performance and validation by note type.

## Implications for This Literature Review
Supports the claim that EHR-note NLP for geriatric syndromes is feasible but under-validated.

## Recommended Follow-up
Extract and review dementia-specific primary studies included in the review.
