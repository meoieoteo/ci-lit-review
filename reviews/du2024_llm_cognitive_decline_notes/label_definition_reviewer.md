# Label Definition Review: du2024_llm_cognitive_decline_notes

## Review Status

Complete.

## Label Summary

The label is section-level cognitive decline evidence in clinical notes.

## Label Type

Manual annotation by trained annotators with expert oversight, inherited from the prior MGB note-section study.

## Target Construct

Evidence from SCD through MCI and dementia, focused on progressive cognitive decline.

## Timing

Notes are from the four years before initial MCI diagnosis in 2019.

## Strengths

The label definition is explicit and excludes transient, reversible, improved, and uncertain cases. Fleiss kappa 0.83 is reported for an agreement set.

## Weaknesses

The label is section-level and derived from a selected cohort with later MCI diagnosis. It is not a general incident dementia endpoint.

## Risk of Label Leakage

Explicit cognitive statements are intended signals for phenotyping. For prospective risk prediction, notes near MCI diagnosis may include workup-related evidence.

## Use in Review

Use for LLM evaluation on a well-described cognitive-note label; do not treat as a new independent label source from Wang 2021.
