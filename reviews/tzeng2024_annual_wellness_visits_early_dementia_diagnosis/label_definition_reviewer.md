# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

Assessment of first MCI/ADRD diagnosis labels and AWV as a diagnosis-opportunity exposure.

## Bottom Line

care_pathway_label. The diagnosis labels are clear as claims-coded first diagnoses, but the paper's main value is showing that labels are affected by care contact.

## Label Summary

First post-index MCI or ADRD diagnosis, analyzed as combined MCI/ADRD, MCI alone, and ADRD alone.

## Target Construct

`future_coded_dementia_diagnosis`; `future_care_pathway`; `future_referral_or_testing` indirectly.

## Label Source and Operational Definition

ADRD uses CMS CCW diagnosis-code algorithm. MCI uses ICD-9-CM 331.83 and ICD-10-CM G31.84 as reported.

## Index Date and Label Timing

First 2018 AWV date for exposed beneficiaries; matched pseudo-index date for non-AWV beneficiaries.

## Positive Case Definition

First MCI or ADRD diagnosis from index through December 31, 2022.

## Negative or Control Definition

No coded MCI/ADRD diagnosis during follow-up before censoring or competing death. This is not a confirmed cognitively normal group.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Prior MCI/ADRD and long-term nursing home residents were excluded. Indeterminate cognitive status is not defined.

## Label Validity Evidence

The study uses established claims algorithms for ADRD and diagnosis codes for MCI. It does not perform new chart or cognitive-test validation.

## Misclassification Risks

MCI is likely undercoded; ADRD code sensitivity/specificity may vary. AWV can make a previously unobserved condition visible.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Central. AWV is explicitly a cognitive-assessment opportunity and is associated with first MCI diagnosis.

## Leakage Risks

For the study's causal association design, AWV is the exposure, not leakage. For this project, post-index AWV/cognitive assessment would be label-observation information, not a pre-index predictor unless available before the cardiology encounter.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Strong as evidence for label observability. A future diagnosis label should account for post-index AWV/PCP/specialty contact.

## Strengths

Clear exposure, index, follow-up, competing-risk handling, and sensitivity analyses.

## Concerns

Claims labels are not disease truth; AWV self-selection remains.

## Missing Information

Actual AWV cognitive assessment content and referral details are not available in claims.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use to justify measuring post-index care contact and cognitive assessment opportunity when interpreting future MCI/ADRD labels.

## Recommended Follow-up

Map local EHR variables for AWV, PCP follow-up, cognitive screening, neurology/psychiatry referral, and testing.
