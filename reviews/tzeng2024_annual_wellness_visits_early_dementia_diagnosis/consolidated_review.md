# Consolidated Review: tzeng2024_annual_wellness_visits_early_dementia_diagnosis

## Consolidation Status

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Tzeng et al. shows that Medicare annual wellness visits are associated with earlier/higher first MCI diagnosis, making AWV a concrete example of diagnosis opportunity affecting cognitive labels.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

This is a direct Section 5 paper and should be cited for care-process-driven label observability.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`downstream_action_bias`; `label_construction`; `problem_framing`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

True cognitive impairment incidence, cardiology-specific screening, NLP, or causal proof that AWVs independently improve outcomes.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

Peer-reviewed journal article. Strong direct relevance, but observational design and claims labels limit causal interpretation.

## Evidence Strength

`strong_direct_evidence`; `methodological_caution`

## Key Contributions

- AWV is associated with first MCI diagnosis.
- The MCI association is stronger than the ADRD association.
- PCP and specialty contact are relevant diagnosis-opportunity variables.

## Key Limitations

Self-selection into AWV, Texas-only fee-for-service Medicare, claims-only labels, and no AWV content data.

## Agreements Across Reviewers

All reviewers agree that this paper directly supports diagnosis-opportunity bias and does not support EHR text claims.

## Tensions or Disagreements Across Reviewers

No major disagreement. The causal meaning of AWV remains cautious.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

First MCI/ADRD diagnosis after index is a care-observed event. Post-index AWV, PCP, and specialty visits can determine whether the label appears.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

Design is strong for an observational claims study; generalizability beyond Texas FFS Medicare requires caution.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

Annual wellness visits are a concrete care-pathway mechanism that can create or accelerate cognitive diagnosis labels.

## Implications for Study Design

If using future MCI/ADRD diagnosis, measure post-index AWV, PCP contact, specialty contact, cognitive testing, censoring, and death.

## Claims to Carry Forward

- Diagnosis labels can be affected by routine preventive care exposure.
- MCI labels may be especially sensitive to detection opportunity.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

- AWV association is not proof of causal diagnosis improvement.
- Claims-coded MCI is not equivalent to adjudicated MCI.

## Open Questions

How should AWV-like events be handled if they occur after the cardiology encounter but before outcome diagnosis?

## Recommended Next Action

Promote to Section 5 supporting evidence in the next state update.
