# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Assessment of observational validation, robustness, and transportability.

## Bottom Line

Good observational design for the question, but not causal proof and not label validation.

## Validation Design Summary

Propensity-score-matched retrospective cohort with Fine-Gray competing-risk models and sensitivity analyses.

## Development and Validation Separation

Not a prediction model.

## Internal Validation

Sensitivity analyses support robustness of the MCI association.

## Temporal Validation

No separate temporal validation; follow-up spans 2018-2022 and includes COVID-era disruption.

## External Validation

No external validation outside Texas fee-for-service Medicare.

## Transportability

Moderate. AWV is a Medicare-wide policy, but Texas FFS population and AWV use may differ from other settings.

## Calibration

Not applicable.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

Not applicable.

## Clinical Utility

The study argues AWVs may support earlier MCI recognition but does not evaluate downstream outcomes or net benefit.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Baseline race/ethnicity and area variables are included, but the main extracted text does not show detailed subgroup effect estimates.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

Two sensitivity analyses support a stronger AWV-MCI association; ADRD association weakens or disappears in one.

## Reporting Completeness

Good reporting of cohort derivation, exposure, outcomes, matching, competing risks, and limitations.

## Reproducibility

Likely reproducible with Medicare claims and supplement code definitions.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Strong for designing diagnosis-opportunity sensitivity analyses.

## Strengths

Large cohort, clear index date, matching, death as competing risk.

## Concerns

Residual self-selection into AWV; claims-only outcomes; limited generalizability.

## Missing Information

Clinic-level AWV content, cognitive testing details, and referral mechanics.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use as direct evidence that observed diagnosis depends on surveillance/contact.

## Recommended Follow-up

Check supplement if exact code sets or pre-COVID sensitivity details are needed.
