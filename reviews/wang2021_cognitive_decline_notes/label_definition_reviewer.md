# Label Definition Review: wang2021_cognitive_decline_notes

## Review Status

Complete.

## Label Summary

The label is section-level evidence of cognitive decline in clinical notes.

## Label Type

Manual annotation by trained annotators with subject matter expert support.

## Target Construct

Progressive cognitive decline likely consistent with or leading to MCI, including subjective cognitive decline, MCI, and dementia.

## Timing

Notes were from the four years before initial MCI diagnosis in 2019. Labels refer to note-section content, not later outcome occurrence.

## Strengths

The definition distinguishes progressive cognitive decline from transient, reversible, improved, or uncertain cognitive findings. Agreement was reported with Fleiss kappa 0.83 on a 50-section set.

## Weaknesses

The cohort is anchored on later MCI diagnosis, and the label is section-level rather than patient-level. EHR MCI diagnosis may be heterogeneous and not necessarily AD-related.

## Risk of Label Leakage

For phenotyping, explicit mentions of cognitive concern are intended signals. For prospective prediction, using notes near diagnosis may capture diagnostic workup rather than preclinical risk.

## Use in Review

Use as a strong example of explicit cognitive-decline note-label definition and annotation. Do not treat as a clean incident dementia outcome.
