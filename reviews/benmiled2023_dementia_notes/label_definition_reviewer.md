# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

Incident dementia case/control label for one-year-ahead prediction.

## Bottom Line

The target is relevant, but the diagnosis label likely reflects care pathways as well as disease.

## Label Summary

Binary incident dementia case status versus matched controls.

## Target Construct

`future_coded_dementia_diagnosis`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

Dementia status is derived from institutional EHR definitions; exact code rules should be checked before citation.

## Index Date and Label Timing

Cases have a disease index date; notes from the final year before index were excluded to create a one-year horizon.

## Positive Case Definition

Incident dementia after excluding prior dementia and MCI.

## Negative or Control Definition

Matched controls without prior dementia/MCI.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Prior dementia and MCI were excluded.

## Label Validity Evidence

The summary does not identify adjudication against a specialist reference standard.

## Misclassification Risks

Controls may include undiagnosed cognitive impairment. Cases may be diagnosis-opportunity positives.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Future coded diagnosis depends on healthcare contact, referral, and documentation.

## Leakage Risks

The one-year exclusion helps reduce immediate diagnostic-workup leakage.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Relevant for future coded diagnosis, not directly for current unmet care need.

## Strengths

Explicit one-year prediction gap and exclusion of known MCI/dementia.

## Concerns

Case-control design and code-derived labels.

## Missing Information

Exact dementia phenotype and follow-up requirements.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Useful for illustrating why future diagnosis labels need diagnosis-opportunity caveats.

## Recommended Follow-up

Source-check code definitions before using as direct label precedent.
