# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

Dementia case/control labels in dissertation EHR modeling.

## Bottom Line

The label is directly relevant but needs source checking before being treated as a reusable reference standard.

## Label Summary

Binary dementia case/control status across observation and prediction windows.

## Target Construct

`future_coded_dementia_diagnosis`; `future_cognitive_impairment`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

EHR-derived dementia case/control construction in INPC data.

## Index Date and Label Timing

Index dates support 0.5- to 1-year prediction horizons.

## Positive Case Definition

Dementia cases defined by dissertation phenotype.

## Negative or Control Definition

Matched controls.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Details require source-checking in the dissertation.

## Label Validity Evidence

No independent adjudicated reference standard is evident from the summary.

## Misclassification Risks

Controls may include unrecognized dementia; cases may reflect documentation intensity.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Future diagnosis is sensitive to referral, testing, and clinician recognition.

## Leakage Risks

Observation/horizon separation helps, but exact handling of notes near diagnosis should be checked.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Conceptually relevant but not cardiology-indexed.

## Strengths

Explicit observation windows and horizons.

## Concerns

Dissertation source and uncertain phenotype details.

## Missing Information

Exact code lists, washouts, and control follow-up.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use as design context rather than a definitive label precedent.

## Recommended Follow-up

Find peer-reviewed chapter/article equivalents if available.
