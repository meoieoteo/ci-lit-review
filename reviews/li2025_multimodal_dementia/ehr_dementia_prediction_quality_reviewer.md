# Specialist Review: EHR Dementia Prediction Quality

## Specialist Scope

Multimodal EHR and notes dissertation models for dementia detection.

## Bottom Line

Highly relevant methods work, but evidence weight is limited by dissertation status and lack of external validation.

## Prediction Task Clarity

Early dementia detection using structured EHR data and notes.

## Index Date, Observation Window, and Prediction Horizon

Observation windows of one to three years and horizons around 0.5 to 1 year.

## Data Source and Cohort Definition

Indiana Network for Patient Care matched case-control data.

## Outcome and Reference Standard

EHR-derived dementia case/control status; exact phenotype requires source checking.

## Comparator or Control Group

Matched controls.

## EHR Text and Structured Data Use

Uses demographics, diagnoses, and lengthy medical notes.

## Missing Data and Feature Availability

Feature availability and note length are central; source-specific missingness needs checking.

## Validation

Nested cross-validation is reported; no external validation identified.

## Calibration, Thresholds, and Clinical Utility

Performance comparisons are emphasized; clinical thresholds and calibration are not established.

## Reporting and Reproducibility

Dissertation provides detail, but model/data reuse is limited.

## Care-Pathway and Downstream-Action Bias

Future diagnosis labels remain care-pathway dependent.

## Implementation Readiness

Promising but not deployment-ready.

## Strengths

Direct dementia task, multimodal inputs, explicit horizons, long-note strategy.

## Concerns

Lower publication weight, no external validation, uncertain label details.

## Missing Information

Phenotype code lists, calibration, threshold operating points.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use for architecture and design ideas more than central clinical evidence.

## Recommended Follow-up

Identify peer-reviewed publications from the dissertation.
