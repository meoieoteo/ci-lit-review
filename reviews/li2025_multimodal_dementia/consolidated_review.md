# Consolidated Review: li2025_multimodal_dementia

## Consolidation Status

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Li 2025 is directly relevant dissertation evidence that multimodal structured EHR plus notes can improve dementia detection, but it should be weighted below peer-reviewed externally validated studies.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

Use this dissertation for design ideas: explicit horizons, long-note content selection, and hybrid fusion of structured and note representations.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`longitudinal_ehr_modeling`; `ehr_text_signal`; `dementia_prediction_performance`; `background_only`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

Do not use as central clinical evidence without acknowledging dissertation status and lack of external validation.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

Direct but lower publication weight because it is a dissertation.

## Evidence Strength

`weak_direct_evidence`; `lower_publication_weight`

## Key Contributions

Multimodal fusion, Longformer-based note modeling, structured-data Random Forest representation, and explicit observation/horizon experiments.

## Key Limitations

No external validation identified, unclear calibration, uncertain label phenotype details, and dissertation status.
