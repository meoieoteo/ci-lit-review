# Consolidated Review: spoto2025_diagnostic_signal_decay_dementia

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

Spoto et al. argues that dementia diagnosis codes can lose specificity across inpatient claims, supporting the review's concern that coded dementia outcomes are shaped by documentation and care-setting processes.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

Direct Section 5 evidence for diagnosis-code variability and specificity loss, but lower publication weight because the source is a preprint.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`downstream_action_bias`; `label_construction`; `diagnosis_code_specificity`; `methodological_caution`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

Validated dementia phenotype performance, outpatient cardiology workflow, EHR text modeling, or true dementia prevalence.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

arXiv preprint. Directly relevant to diagnosis-code outcome interpretation, but should be cited cautiously and preferably alongside peer-reviewed evidence.

## Evidence Strength

`direct_but_lower_publication_weight`; `methodological_caution`

## Key Contributions

The paper shows that non-specific dementia codes dominate inpatient dementia-coded hospitalizations and that code-pattern similarity varies geographically and demographically.

## Key Limitations

Inpatient claims only; no chart validation; ecological geographic analysis; cannot distinguish clinical reclassification from administrative coding behavior.

## Agreements Across Reviewers

All reviewers agree the paper is useful for Section 5 label-observation bias and not an NLP, prediction, or EHR schema methods paper.

## Tensions or Disagreements Across Reviewers

No substantive disagreement. The key caution is publication weight and limited setting transportability.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

Future dementia diagnosis codes should preserve setting, timestamp, and code specificity. Non-specific code endpoints are weaker clinical labels than etiologically specific endpoints.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

The paper should motivate local validation of code specificity and first-code source, especially if future diagnosis is used as an endpoint.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

Hospital contact and coding convention can determine whether and how dementia is observed administratively.

## Implications for Study Design

Track whether an outcome code first appears in inpatient, outpatient, emergency, problem-list, or specialist contexts; run sensitivity analyses by code specificity and source setting.

## Claims to Carry Forward

Diagnosis-code specificity is itself an outcome-quality issue.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

Generalizing exact transition rates or geographic effects beyond Medicare inpatient claims.

## Open Questions

How often do local dementia codes shift from specific to non-specific categories, and do notes preserve more precise cognitive evidence?

## Recommended Next Action

Use as lower-weight Section 5 support and as a prompt for local outcome-provenance fields.
