# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope
Assessment of validation, calibration, thresholding, transportability, and reproducibility lessons from Lu et al. 2026.

## Bottom Line
`underreported_validation`. The EHR dementia prediction literature has sparse external validation, sparse calibration, and weak clinical utility reporting.

## Validation Design Summary
The review includes 434 developed models and 155 external validations, but only 47 developed models were externally validated.

## Development and Validation Separation
Frequently weak or unclear in the included literature.

## Internal Validation
Commonly reported, but high risk of bias remains.

## Temporal Validation
Not a strong consistent feature across the literature.

## External Validation
Sparse. External validation is one of the major gaps.

## Transportability
Limited by heterogeneous settings, coding practices, target definitions, and lack of external validation.

## Calibration
Sparse. Calibration was reported for only 69 models across 9 studies.

## Thresholds and Operating Points
Rarely reported or clinically justified.

## Clinical Utility
Weak. AUROC is commonly reported, but action-linked clinical utility is rarely established.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation
Not a central extracted strength.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses
Limited by heterogeneity and poor reporting.

## Reporting Completeness
Weak across the field and a major barrier to evaluation.

## Reproducibility
Limited. Many papers do not report enough to reproduce or validate models.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring
Very high as a negative design lesson. Our study should build validation, calibration, thresholds, and reporting into the protocol.

## Strengths
- Large systematic review.
- Uses established reporting and bias frameworks.
- Concrete counts for calibration and external validation gaps.

## Concerns
- Does not define the project's validation solution.
- Published-report limitations constrain the review.

## Missing Information
Detailed model-level validation patterns require supplementary extraction.

## Implications for This Literature Review
This paper should anchor the validation, calibration, and clinical utility sections.

## Recommended Follow-up
Use alongside John et al. to define minimum reporting and external validation requirements.
