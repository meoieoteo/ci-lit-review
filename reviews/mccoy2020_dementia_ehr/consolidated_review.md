# Consolidated Review: mccoy2020_dementia_ehr

## Consolidation Status

consolidated_complete

## One-Sentence Takeaway

McCoy 2020 shows that cognitive symptom burden in discharge notes is associated with earlier future dementia coding, but it is not a deployable outpatient risk model.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

Use as direct longitudinal evidence that routine EHR text can contain dementia-relevant signal years before coded diagnosis.

## What This Paper Should Be Cited For

`ehr_text_signal`; `dementia_prediction_performance`; `longitudinal_ehr_modeling`; `label_construction`

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

Do not cite for outpatient cardiology deployment, calibration, or utility thresholds.

## Publication Status and Evidence Weight

Peer-reviewed direct evidence with moderate weight for association and lower weight for the target cardiology setting.

## Evidence Strength

`moderate_direct_evidence`; `methodological_caution`

## Key Contributions

Large cohort, long follow-up, competing-risk modeling, and interpretable discharge-note cognitive symptom exposure.

## Key Limitations

Inpatient-only index, future coded diagnosis outcome, no calibration, and diagnosis-opportunity bias.

## Agreements Across Reviewers

All reviewers agree the paper is direct EHR-text dementia evidence but not a full prediction-model validation.

## Tensions or Disagreements Across Reviewers

No major disagreement.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

Future coded diagnosis is not equivalent to true disease onset. Discharge-note symptoms may reflect already recognized impairment.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

Second-system/subgroup support helps, but outpatient transport is untested.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

Follow-up diagnosis depends on care contact and diagnostic attention.

## Implications for Study Design

Our study should distinguish text evidence of current impairment from future coded diagnosis, and should report calibration if producing risk scores.

## Claims to Carry Forward

Cognitive symptoms documented in routine notes can precede dementia diagnosis codes by years.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

Hazard association should not be presented as clinical prediction readiness.

## Open Questions

Would outpatient cardiology notes show similar cognitive-symptom signal?

## Recommended Next Action

Cite for longitudinal note signal with inpatient and coded-outcome caveats.
