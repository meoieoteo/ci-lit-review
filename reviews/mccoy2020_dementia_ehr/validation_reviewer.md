# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Validation of a discharge-note cognitive symptom score as a risk-stratifying exposure.

## Bottom Line

Large-sample association is robust, but this is not a full clinical prediction validation.

## Validation Design Summary

Retrospective cohort with competing-risk regression and replication-style analysis in another hospital system/subgroup.

## Development and Validation Separation

The NLP score was prespecified/validated previously rather than optimized for this dementia outcome.

## Internal Validation

Large cohort analysis with adjusted estimates.

## Temporal Validation

Follow-up extends up to eight years; no calendar-time validation split.

## External Validation

Second hospital-system analysis provides partial external support.

## Transportability

Moderate for inpatient discharge settings; uncertain for outpatient cardiology.

## Calibration

Not a risk-model calibration study.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

No clinical operating threshold for risk score use.

## Clinical Utility

Not established.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Age subgroup is noted; broader fairness evaluation is not central.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

Competing-risk approach is appropriate for mortality.

## Reporting Completeness

Good for association; less complete for implementation.

## Reproducibility

Term-score approach is simpler than opaque models but requires term-list access.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Moderate for longitudinal evidence; low for direct deployment.

## Strengths

Large sample, long follow-up, competing-risk model.

## Concerns

No calibration, no threshold utility, inpatient-only exposure.

## Missing Information

Performance as a standalone prediction model.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use for association evidence, not model-readiness claims.

## Recommended Follow-up

Separate hazard association from prediction performance in synthesis.
