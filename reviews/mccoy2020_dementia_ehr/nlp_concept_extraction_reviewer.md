# Specialist Review: NLP Concept Extraction

## Specialist Scope

Cognitive symptom scoring from discharge notes.

## Bottom Line

The NLP method is interpretable and longitudinally informative, but it is symptom burden scoring rather than rich cognitive concept extraction.

## NLP Task and Clinical Concept Summary

Term-based cognitive symptom score from discharge notes.

## Note Sources and Text Window

Inpatient discharge notes at index hospitalization.

## Concept Inventory Relevance

Relevant to cognition terms; less detailed for function, caregiver, social, or care-navigation concepts.

## Annotation and Reference Standard

The tool was validated previously; this paper uses it as an exposure.

## Extraction Method

Prespecified/expanded term list and percentage of terms appearing in the note.

## Context Handling

Context handling should be checked; term presence may not fully distinguish negation or historical/family mentions.

## Temporality and Leakage

Text precedes future diagnosis codes but may contain already recognized cognitive symptoms.

## Evaluation Design

Association with incident dementia over follow-up rather than NLP extraction accuracy in this paper.

## Error Analysis

Not central in summary.

## Portability and Implementation

Term score is relatively implementable but discharge-note-specific.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Moderate for cognitive text signal; low for outpatient cardiology note source.

## Strengths

Simple interpretable exposure and long follow-up.

## Concerns

Limited concept granularity and context uncertainty.

## Missing Information

Assertion handling and term list details.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Use as evidence that even simple cognitive-symptom text signals predict future coding.

## Recommended Follow-up

Compare symptom terms with outpatient cognitive/frailty concept inventory.
