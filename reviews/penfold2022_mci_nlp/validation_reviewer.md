# Specialist Review: Validation

## Specialist Scope

Validation of an NLP concept model for MCI identification.

## Bottom Line

Useful pilot validation, but performance is not sufficient for broad screening.

## Validation Design Summary

Trained on ACT plus 60% of general-population patients; validated on remaining 40%.

## Development and Validation Separation

The held-out general-population split provides separation, though feature engineering came from annotated data.

## Internal Validation

Tenfold cross-validation is reported.

## Temporal Validation

No temporal validation.

## External Validation

No external health-system validation.

## Transportability

Unproven across EHRs, note types, and specialties.

## Calibration

Not a central reported strength.

## Thresholds and Operating Points

High-specificity cutoff reported; sensitivity was extremely low.

## Clinical Utility

Could support rule-in review but not reliable case finding.

## Subgroup and Fairness Evaluation

Not sufficient for deployment decisions.

## Robustness and Sensitivity Analyses

Limited pilot scope.

## Reporting Completeness

Core performance is reported; implementation details require source checking.

## Reproducibility

Concept inventory helps reproducibility but local phrase mapping would be needed.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Moderate as a pilot method; low as a ready model.

## Strengths

Held-out validation and interpretable features.

## Concerns

Low sensitivity, no external validation, selective testing.

## Missing Information

Calibration and decision-curve evidence.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Treat as feasibility evidence with strong operating-point caveats.

## Recommended Follow-up

Do not cite AUC without noting threshold limitations.
