# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

MCI label based on cognitive test thresholds.

## Bottom Line

The label is clinically clearer than diagnosis-code-only dementia labels, but selective testing limits interpretation.

## Label Summary

Binary MCI status from MMSE/MoCA threshold, with ACT CASI converted to MMSE.

## Target Construct

`current_undocumented_cognitive_impairment`; `current_unmet_cognitive_care_need`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

Standardized cognitive assessment scores; positive threshold at or below 26.

## Index Date and Label Timing

Index is anchored to cognitive assessment timing. Notes near the assessment require careful timing review.

## Positive Case Definition

Low cognitive assessment score.

## Negative or Control Definition

Assessment score above threshold.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Patients with prior dementia/AD and related exclusions were removed.

## Label Validity Evidence

Cognitive tests provide stronger evidence than codes, though they are screening instruments rather than adjudicated diagnosis.

## Misclassification Risks

False positives from non-dementia causes of low scores; false negatives from high-functioning patients or test variability.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Testing was not universal, so being tested may reflect clinician concern.

## Leakage Risks

Notes documenting the same concern that triggered testing could inflate apparent signal.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Useful label analogy, but not cardiology-anchored.

## Strengths

Explicit threshold and standardized tests.

## Concerns

Selective testing and threshold-based case definition.

## Missing Information

Exact temporal separation between note evidence and testing should be source-checked.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Supports cognitive-test labels but warns that tested populations are selected.

## Recommended Follow-up

Use as a label-quality comparator against code-based dementia studies.
