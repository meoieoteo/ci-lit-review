# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

ICU in-hospital mortality label.

## Bottom Line

The label is clear for its ICU task but not relevant to dementia target construction.

## Label Summary

Binary in-hospital mortality.

## Target Construct

`other`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

MIMIC-III hospital outcome.

## Index Date and Label Timing

ICU admission with first 48 hours as predictors.

## Positive Case Definition

Death during hospitalization.

## Negative or Control Definition

Survival to discharge.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Patients without notes were excluded.

## Label Validity Evidence

Mortality is comparatively objective.

## Misclassification Risks

Low relative to dementia labels.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Outcome is affected by ICU treatment processes but not by diagnosis opportunity in the dementia sense.

## Leakage Risks

First-48-hour features must precede the outcome; late notes could be problematic if outcome occurs early.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Low for labels.

## Strengths

Clear endpoint and time origin.

## Concerns

Excludes patients without notes.

## Missing Information

Outcome timing relative to notes for early deaths.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Useful only as a clean-label contrast to dementia labels.

## Recommended Follow-up

Do not cite for dementia labels.
