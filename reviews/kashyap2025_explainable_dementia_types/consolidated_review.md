## One-Sentence Takeaway

Kashyap 2025 is useful for LLM-aided interpretable note features, but its dementia-subtype target is not the project's early risk target.

## Bottom Line for This Literature Review

Use this paper for method context on LLM-aided concept engineering and interpretability, not for dementia risk-model evidence.

## What This Paper Should Not Be Cited For

Peer-reviewed article with moderate method weight and lower target relevance.

## Key Contributions

GPT-4-aided concept generation, concept-vector classification, baseline comparisons, and reported efficiency gains.

## Key Limitations

Subtype target, single-site data, no temporal horizon, limited temporal/context handling.

## Label, Target, and Timing Takeaways

Dementia subtype classification should not be merged with detection or future risk.

## Validation and Transportability Takeaways

Internal comparison is useful; external transport remains untested.

## Bias and Downstream-Action Takeaways

Subtype labels likely depend on specialist workup and documentation.

## Implications for Study Design

LLMs may help generate auditable features, but our labels and time windows must be defined separately.

## Claims to Carry Forward

LLM-aided concept features can outperform n-gram and direct-LLM baselines in a dementia-note task.

## Claims to Treat Cautiously

Subtype accuracy does not imply early detection utility.

## Open Questions

Which concepts overlap with pre-cardiology cognitive/frailty signals?

## Recommended Next Action

Mine concept families only if needed for the note-feature inventory.
