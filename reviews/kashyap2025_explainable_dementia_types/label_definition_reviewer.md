# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope

Dementia-type classification label.

## Bottom Line

The target is clinically meaningful for subtype phenotyping but not for early risk or unmet-care detection.

## Label Summary

Multiclass dementia type.

## Target Construct

`prevalent_known_dementia`; `other`

## Label Source and Operational Definition

Clinical dementia-type labels in a single-hospital dataset.

## Index Date and Label Timing

The task is not framed around a prediction index or horizon.

## Positive Case Definition

Each class represents a dementia type.

## Negative or Control Definition

Not applicable in the same way as binary risk prediction.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases

Unclear from summary.

## Label Validity Evidence

Subtype labels depend on clinical documentation and workup.

## Misclassification Risks

Dementia subtype is hard to classify and may change with further diagnostic workup.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias

Subtype labels depend heavily on specialist evaluation and documentation.

## Leakage Risks

Notes may contain explicit subtype diagnoses.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring

Low for target definition; moderate for feature extraction.

## Strengths

Clinically interpretable subtype target.

## Concerns

Subtype classification is not the project's primary target.

## Missing Information

Exact subtype label provenance.

## Implications for This Literature Review

Do not conflate subtype classification with early detection.

## Recommended Follow-up

Cite for concept-feature methods, not target definition.
