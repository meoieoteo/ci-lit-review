# NLP / Concept Extraction Review: wornow2023_shaky_foundations_ehr_fm

## Review Status

Complete.

## NLP Task

The paper reviews clinical language models across many NLP tasks, including named entity recognition, relation extraction, document classification, question answering, summarization, and generation.

## NLP Methods

The review covers language-model and foundation-model approaches rather than one extraction pipeline.

## Concept Handling

The key critique is that traditional NLP benchmarks may show linguistic capability without proving clinical usefulness in a deployment context.

## Strengths

The paper is useful for evaluating clinical NLP claims because it separates task performance from health-system value and highlights weak correlation between automated generation metrics and human judgment.

## Limitations

It is not a detailed review of dementia-specific concept extraction, negation, temporality, section detection, or phenotype definitions.

## Use in Review

Use as a high-level caution that clinical NLP and LLM evaluations should be grounded in the intended clinical decision, not only in benchmark performance.
