# Label Definition Review: hong2020_cognitive_concerns_nlp

## Review Status

Complete.

## Label Summary

The outcome is presence or absence of cognitive concerns, described as signs of dementia, assigned by expert clinician review of each patient's EHR record.

## Label Type

Clinician-adjudicated EHR record review.

## Target Construct

Broad cognitive concern rather than a narrow dementia diagnosis. The positive class appears to include subjective cognitive concern, MCI, and dementia.

## Timing

Labels were based on EHR records from January 1, 2018 through December 31, 2018. The paper is not framed as pre-index prospective prediction.

## Strengths

The label is not merely an ICD-code proxy. Expert review is a meaningful strength for a case-finding NLP study.

## Weaknesses

The short paper does not provide an annotation guide, adjudication details, rater agreement, criteria for subjective concern versus MCI versus dementia, or how conflicting evidence was handled.

## Risk of Label Leakage

High risk if notes contain explicit diagnostic statements or cognitive-assessment language from the same window used for labeling. This is acceptable for phenotyping but limits claims about prospective prediction.

## Use in Review

Use as evidence for note-based cognitive-concern phenotyping. Do not use as evidence for a well-specified prospective dementia onset label.
