# Specialist Review: Label Definition

## Specialist Scope
Assessment of how John et al. 2022 informs dementia label definition, reporting, timing, and operational reproducibility.

## Bottom Line
`usable_with_caveats`. The paper is valuable for showing that target and timing details are often underreported, but it is not itself a validated dementia-label recipe.

## Label Summary
The labels concern incident dementia or ADRD outcomes in published prediction models and external validation databases.

## Target Construct
- `future_coded_dementia_diagnosis`
- possible `future_cognitive_impairment`, depending on source model definitions

The dominant construct is future recorded dementia in observational health data, not current unmet cognitive care need.

## Label Source and Operational Definition
partial. The paper evaluates whether models report enough target/outcome detail, but the summary does not establish that the underlying labels are clinically adjudicated.

## Index Date and Label Timing
weak in the broader model literature. Only a subset of models reported index date and predictor time window, which is directly relevant to leakage and reproducibility.

## Positive Case Definition
partial. Positive definitions vary by model and database; the paper is more about reporting and validation feasibility than label adjudication.

## Negative or Control Definition
not_reported in the current summary. This remains important because absence of dementia codes can mean undocumented impairment rather than true absence.

## Indeterminate or Excluded Cases
not_reported in the current summary.

## Label Validity Evidence
partial. External validation tests transportability of model performance, not necessarily clinical validity of the dementia labels.

## Misclassification Risks
Likely present because observational dementia labels can depend on diagnosis, coding, care access, and follow-up.

## Downstream-Action and Diagnosis-Opportunity Bias
partial. The paper does not directly analyze diagnosis-opportunity bias, but its findings imply that timing and outcome reporting are necessary before such bias can be assessed.

## Leakage Risks
High relevance. Missing index-date and predictor-window reporting makes leakage impossible to rule out for many models.

## Fit to Pre-Cardiology Risk Scoring
Useful as a warning and reporting standard. It does not provide a cardiology-specific label, but it supports strict reporting of encounter start, pre-index predictors, and post-index outcome windows.

## Strengths
- Makes label timing and model specification concrete validation requirements.
- Directly supports the need for reproducible target definitions.

## Concerns
- Does not establish that any reviewed dementia label is clinically valid for unmet cognitive care need.
- Does not separate disease state from diagnosis opportunity.

## Missing Information
- Control definitions.
- Code lists and outcome algorithms in the current summary.
- Handling of indeterminate cognitive evidence.

## Implications for This Literature Review
Use this paper to argue that label timing and operational detail are prerequisites for external validation and downstream bias assessment.

## Recommended Follow-up
- Source-check model reporting criteria and exact counts before manuscript citation.
- Pair this paper with Lu et al. when motivating the label-definition reviewer.
