# horvitz1987_beliefs_actions_resources

## Bibliographic Record
Horvitz, Eric J. "Reasoning About Beliefs and Actions Under Computational Resource Constraints." Proceedings of the Third Conference on Uncertainty in Artificial Intelligence, 1987. https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.2759.

## Plain-Language Summary
This paper argues that reasoning systems should consider the costs and benefits of computation and knowledge acquisition when complete normative decision analysis is infeasible. It introduces ideas related to real-time rationality and value-guided approximation.

## Structured Summary
